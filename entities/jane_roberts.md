# Jane Roberts (Ruburt)

**Full Name:** Jane Roberts  
**Entity Name:** Ruburt (Seth's name for Jane's full soul-identity)  
**Born:** May 8, 1929 — Saratoga Springs, New York  
**Died:** September 5, 1984 — Elmira, New York  
**Role:** Channel / Author / Poet  

---

## Background

Jane Roberts was an American author, poet, and psychic who became the primary channel for the Seth Material. Before the Seth sessions began in 1963, she was already an established creative writer with published short stories and was working on a novel. Her first book of poetry had been accepted for publication.

Jane grew up in difficult circumstances — her mother was an invalid and her early life was marked by poverty and instability. These experiences deeply shaped her intellectual and spiritual temperament: skeptical, independent, fiercely questioning, and unwilling to accept authority without examination.

She met Robert Butts in the late 1950s, and the two moved to Elmira, New York, where they lived for most of their lives together, conducting the Seth sessions from their apartment.

## Role as Channel

Jane first began experimenting with a Ouija board in the fall of 1963 as research for a book she was writing. Contact with the entity who identified himself as Seth began almost immediately. Within weeks, Jane began to "hear" Seth's words directly and to speak them aloud rather than using the board. Full trance channeling followed.

Jane's trance states varied considerably across the twenty years of sessions:
- **Light trance:** Jane remained somewhat aware of her surroundings, could sometimes remember what was said
- **Deep trance:** Jane had no recall of session content; her voice, posture, and manner changed dramatically
- **Eyes-open sessions:** Often conducted with Seth using Jane's eyes to look directly at Rob

Jane approached the Seth Material with a combination of trust and ongoing skepticism. She never fully abandoned her questioning stance, and this tension — between her rational, literary mind and the extraordinary nature of what was happening — is evident throughout the published works.

## Personal Writings

In addition to channeling Seth's books, Jane wrote extensively in her own voice:

- *The Seth Material* (1970) — her account of the sessions' origins and early content
- *The Coming of Seth* (1966/reissued) — early documentation
- *Adventures in Consciousness* (1975) — her theory of "aspect psychology"
- *Psychic Politics* (1976) — exploration of her own psychic experiences
- *The God of Jane* (1981) — personal account of her relationship with her own creativity and inner guidance
- *If We Live Again* (1982) — poems about reincarnation
- Multiple novels and poetry collections

## Health and Later Life

In her later years, Jane suffered from severe arthritis and related conditions that increasingly confined her to a wheelchair and eventually to bed. The nature of this illness — whether it represented her beliefs at work, a chosen challenge, or something else entirely — was a subject of deep personal examination in her later writings and in the Seth sessions themselves.

Seth addressed Jane's health issues directly in many sessions, and the tension between Seth's teachings about beliefs creating reality and Jane's physical deterioration is one of the most humanly complex threads in the entire corpus.

Jane died on September 5, 1984, in Elmira, New York, in a nursing home. Rob was with her.

## Trance Characteristics

- Seth's voice was distinctly different from Jane's natural voice — deeper, more deliberate, differently accented
- Jane's eyes would typically close at trance onset, then open in a characteristically different way as Seth
- Physical gestures changed: Seth used more deliberate hand gestures for emphasis
- Sessions could last from thirty minutes to several hours
- Jane sometimes reported partial awareness during sessions; other times complete amnesia
