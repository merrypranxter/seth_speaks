## Secondary Entities Mentioned by Seth

### Frank Watts
**Type:** Previous physical personality of Seth  
**Period:** Approximately 1600s, Denmark  
**Context:** Seth identifies Frank Watts as one of his most recent physical incarnations. Frank Watts also appeared briefly in the very earliest Ouija board experiments before Seth fully took over. Seth describes him as a philosopher and teacher.  
**Sessions:** Early Sessions, various; Seth Speaks sessions discussing Seth's own nature

---

### The Speakers
**Type:** Non-physical group / class of teachers  
**Context:** Seth identifies himself as one of the "Speakers" — a class of consciousness whose purpose is to communicate inner knowledge to physical beings across all ages and civilizations. The Speakers work through dreams, inspiration, and direct communication with those who are receptive. They have been responsible for many of humanity's greatest insights across history.  
**Sessions:** Seth Speaks, multiple sessions; Early Sessions

---

### Sumari
**Type:** Family of consciousness / entity grouping  
**Context:** Seth describes various "families of consciousness" that cut across physical incarnations. Sumari is one such family — characterized by creativity, artistic temperament, love of beauty, and an outsider perspective. Jane was identified as Sumari. Seth channeled "Sumari songs" through Jane on several occasions — vocalizations in what Seth described as the Sumari language.  
**Sessions:** Seth Speaks; various personal sessions

---

### Seth Two (Seth II)
**Type:** Communicating entity, presented as a higher-level source  
**Context:** On certain occasions, Jane channeled a different entity — referred to as Seth Two or Seth II — who appeared to operate at a level even further removed from physical reality than Seth himself. Seth Two's communications were notably more abstract and less personal than Seth's, and Seth himself acknowledged Seth Two as representing a higher organizational level.  
**Sessions:** Early Sessions; mentioned in Seth Speaks

---

### William James
**Type:** Historical figure / communicating personality  
**Context:** Jane Roberts separately channeled the psychologist and philosopher William James (1842–1910) in a series of sessions distinct from the Seth sessions. This material was published as *The Afterdeath Journal of an American Philosopher* (1978). Seth occasionally referenced James in his own sessions.  
**Relationship to Seth Material:** Parallel channel, different entity, same medium
